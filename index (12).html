Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about college planning, financial aid, and how College Funding Finders can help your family.

What is College Funding Finders?

College Funding Finders is a resource for U.S. students and families navigating the college selection and funding process. We provide access to powerful college search, financial aid optimization, and comparison tools through our partnership with Collegiate Funding Solutions, a trusted platform used by financial advisors nationwide.

Is College Funding Finders free to use?

Yes, you can explore our college search tools and access general information at no cost. Some advanced features within the Collegiate Funding Solutions platform may require registration or subscription, but basic exploration is free.

What is Expected Family Contribution (EFC)?

The Expected Family Contribution is a number calculated using federal methodology that represents how much your family is expected to contribute toward college costs for one academic year. Schools use your EFC to determine your eligibility for need-based financial aid, including grants, scholarships, work-study, and loans.

How accurate are the financial aid estimates?

Our calculations are based on federal financial aid methodology and official college data. While these provide helpful estimates for planning, actual financial aid packages can vary. Each college has its own policies, and your final aid offer will depend on information provided through the official FAFSA and each school's individual review process.

What's the difference between sticker price and net price?

Sticker price is the published cost of attendance (tuition, fees, room, board) before any financial aid. Net price is what your family actually pays after grants and scholarships are applied. Many families pay significantly less than the sticker price, which is why comparing net prices—not sticker prices—is essential for making informed decisions.

Can College Funding Finders help with scholarship searches?

Our tools help you understand institutional aid (grants and scholarships from the colleges themselves) and how different schools meet financial need. For outside scholarship searches, we recommend complementing our tools with dedicated scholarship databases and your high school's guidance resources.

When should we start using these tools?

We recommend starting your college search during your student's junior year of high school, or even earlier for initial exploration. The financial aid optimizer is most useful when you have a sense of your current financial situation and are ready to start comparing realistic costs across different schools.

Does College Funding Finders provide financial or admissions advice?

College Funding Finders provides educational tools and information to help you understand your options. We do not provide personalized financial advice, investment guidance, or admission counseling. For specific advice tailored to your situation, we recommend consulting with a qualified financial advisor or college planning professional.
